B.C 3500-3000

Cultivation of rice and domestication of silk worms

Early agricultural practices began in the Yellow and Yangtze river valleys. This period marked the foundational domestication of silkworms and the rise of rice farming.

B.C. 1766-1022

The period of the Shang Dynasty

The Shang Dynasty established one of China's earliest formal civilizations. They are known for advanced bronze metallurgy and the use of oracle bones.

B.C. 1400-1300

The development of the Chinese character writing

Oracle bone script evolved into a mature writing system during this time. These early characters were primarily used for divination and administrative record-keeping.

B.C. 1122-221

The period of the Zhou Dynasty

The Zhou Dynasty overthrew the Shang and established the long-lasting "Mandate of Heaven" concept. This era saw significant feudal development and the birth of major philosophical schools.

B.C. 551-479

Confucius teaches philosophy

Confucius developed a system of ethics focusing on social harmony and respect for authority. His teachings, emphasizing filial piety and ritual, became the foundation of Chinese education.

B.C. 221-206

The period of the Qin dynasty

Qin Shi Huang unified China, ending the warring states period and establishing the first empire. This dynasty implemented strict legalist policies and standardized writing and currency.

B.C. 212

The burning of the books and execution of scholars under Zheng

Emperor Qin Shi Huang ordered the destruction of historical texts to suppress dissent. This action aimed to consolidate power by eliminating opposing intellectual thought.

B.C. 215-206

The building of the first Great Wall

The Qin dynasty connected existing border fortifications to protect against northern invasions. This massive construction project utilized massive labor forces and laid the groundwork for future walls.

B.C. 206

Liu Bang founds Han Dynasty

Liu Bang overcame rival forces to establish the long-lasting Han Dynasty after the fall of the Qin. He eased strict legalist laws, adopting Confucian principles to govern the empire.

A.D. 960-1279

The period of the Song Dynasty

The Song Dynasty is renowned for advancements in technology, literature, and art. This period experienced significant economic growth, including the introduction of paper money.

A.D. 1208-1215

Genghis Khan conquers China

Genghis Khan led Mongol forces to break through the northern defenses of the Jin dynasty. This conquest marked the beginning of foreign, non-Han rule over parts of China.

A.D. 1264-1294

Kublai Khan rules China

Kublai Khan established the Yuan Dynasty and moved the capital to Beijing. He promoted trade and cultural exchange along the Silk Road during his reign.

A.D. 1275

Marco Polo at Kublai Khan's court

The Venetian explorer arrived in China and served in the court of Kublai Khan. His detailed accounts later introduced many Europeans to the wonders of Chinese civilization.

A.D. 1582-1610

Father Matteo Ricci, missionary in China

Matteo Ricci was a Jesuit priest who successfully introduced European science to the Ming court. He adopted Chinese customs, facilitating a unique cross-cultural dialogue.

A.D. 1900

The Boxer Rebellion

A violent anti-foreign, anti-colonial uprising took place, targeting missionaries and foreign legations. The rebellion was ultimately suppressed by an eight-nation alliance of foreign powers.

A.D. 1908

Death of Empress Dowager Cixi

Empress Cixi passed away, leaving a power vacuum at the end of the Qing dynasty. Her death accelerated the collapse of imperial rule in China.

A.D. 1924

Formation of the Republic of China

The Republic of China was strengthened through the reorganization of the Kuomintang (Nationalist Party). This period saw efforts to modernize and unify the country against warlords.

A.D. 1937

Japan invades China

The Marco Polo Bridge Incident triggered a full-scale invasion by Japan, beginning the Second Sino-Japanese War. This war forced the Nationalists and Communists into a uneasy united front.

A.D. 1949

Establishment of Red China

The Chinese Communist Party, led by Mao Zedong, won the civil war. The People's Republic of China was officially proclaimed on October 1st.
