A.D. 718–1492

The Reconquista of Spain

A centuries-long series of military campaigns by Christian kingdoms to recapture territory from Muslim Moors. It concluded with the fall of Granada in 1492.

A.D. 1453

Fall of Constantinople

The Byzantine Empire came to an end when the Ottoman Turks captured its capital. This event forced Europeans to find new sea routes to the East.

A.D. 1455

The Gutenberg Bible

Johannes Gutenberg completed the first major book printed using movable metal type in the West. This innovation sparked a revolution in the spread of knowledge.

A.D. 1492

Columbus Discovers the New World

Sailing under the Spanish flag, Christopher Columbus landed in the Americas while seeking a route to Asia. This encounter permanently linked the Eastern and Western Hemispheres.

A.D. 1517

Luther's 95 Theses

Martin Luther famously posted his grievances against Catholic Church practices on the door of the Castle Church in Wittenberg. This act effectively launched the Protestant Reformation.

A.D. 1521

Cortes Conquers Mexico

Spanish conquistador Hernán Cortés led the expedition that caused the fall of the Aztec Empire. This victory brought large portions of mainland Mexico under Spanish rule.

A.D. 1543

On the Revolutions of the Celestial Spheres

Nicolaus Copernicus published his heliocentric theory, placing the Sun rather than the Earth at the center of the universe. It is considered a landmark work in the Scientific Revolution.

A.D. 1545

Opening of the Council of Trent

The Catholic Church convened this council to address the challenges of the Reformation and clarify its doctrines. This marked the official beginning of the Counter-Reformation.

A.D. 1616

Death of Cervantes and Shakespeare

Two of the world’s greatest literary figures passed away during this year. Their works, such as Don Quixote and Hamlet, remain foundations of Western literature.

A.D. 1640

Revolution in England

Tensions between King Charles I and Parliament escalated into an armed conflict over governance and religion. This period began the struggle that would temporarily end the English monarchy.

A.D. 1649

Execution of King Charles I

Following his defeat in the Civil War, Charles I was tried and beheaded for high treason. England was subsequently declared a republic known as the Commonwealth.

A.D. 1689

The Glorious Revolution

King James II was overthrown and replaced by William III and Mary II in a largely bloodless transition. The event established the supremacy of Parliament over the British Crown.

A.D. 1776

The American Revolution

The thirteen American colonies declared their independence from British rule. The resulting war led to the formation of the United States of America.

A.D. 1789

The French Revolution

Citizens of France overthrew the absolute monarchy and dismantled the feudal system. It introduced the world to the ideals of "liberty, equality, and fraternity."
